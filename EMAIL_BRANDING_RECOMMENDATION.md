## ğŸ¨ Recommended Changes

### Option A: Minimal Branding (Recommended)

**Header:** 
- Remove logo or make it very small (optional)
- Just start with the email content directly

**Body:**
- Keep it personal and recruiter-signed
- No branding in the body

**Footer:**
- Very small, subtle line: "Sent via CoreflowHR" or "Powered by CoreflowHR"
- Keep unsubscribe link (legal requirement)
- Remove heavy branding elements (tagline, "Modern Recruitment OS", etc.)

### Option B: No Branding (Ultra-Personal)

**Header:** 
- No logo, no branding

**Body:**
- Completely plain, looks like direct email from recruiter

**Footer:**
- Only legal requirements (unsubscribe)
- No company mention at all
- Maximum personalization

## ğŸ’¡ My Specific Recommendation for CoreflowHR

I recommend **Option A (Minimal Branding)** because:

1. **You're B2B SaaS**: Your customers (recruiters) want to appear professional, but your brand should be subtle
2. **Best Response Rates**: Personal emails with minimal branding perform best
3. **Industry Standard**: Matches what candidates expect from modern recruitment tools
4. **Brand Building**: Still builds awareness without being intrusive
5. **Flexibility**: You could even make it configurable (let users choose)

## Implementation Options

### Option 1: Make It Configurable (Best Long-Term)
- Add a setting: "Email Branding Level"
  - Full branding (current)
  - Minimal branding (recommended)
  - No branding (plain)
- Let recruiters choose what works for their brand

### Option 2: Default to Minimal, Allow Customization
- Default to minimal branding
- Allow users to customize footer text
- Keep it simple and professional

### Option 3: Smart Default Based on Context
- Screening/Outreach: Minimal branding (more personal)
- Interview/Offer: Slightly more branding (more formal)
- Rejection: Minimal branding (sensitive, needs personal touch)
